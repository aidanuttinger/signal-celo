Chapter 05 · Lead Reactivation

The Warm List Playbook

Turning cold conversations, past proposals, and dormant contacts into signed retainer clients.

The Opportunity

Every business has a graveyard of almost-deals — proposals that were sent and never followed up on, LinkedIn connections who expressed interest and then went quiet, past clients who churned for reasons that may no longer apply. These contacts already know CELO exists. The barrier to re-engagement is lower than any cold outreach. Start here before spending a single hour on new outbound.

The Three Pools

Who's in your warm list

Pool 1: Past Proposals That Went Cold
Every proposal sent in the last 24 months with no signed contract is a warm lead. The timing may have been wrong. The budget may have been tighter. The decision maker may have changed. A brief, no-pressure check-in reactivates a significant percentage of these.
Pool 2: Past Clients Who Churned
Clients who left aren't necessarily lost. They may have outgrown a previous solution, been unhappy with a specific team member, or simply needed a break. A direct, honest conversation — acknowledging the past and presenting what's changed — can win these back at a high conversion rate.
Pool 3: LinkedIn Connections & Warm Referrals Never Followed Up
Every "we should talk sometime" that was never scheduled. Every referral introduction that went cold. Every connection who liked a post but never got a personal message. This pool is often the largest and easiest to convert — they already have positive associations with CELO.
The Reactivation Sequence

Four touches. One signature.

1
Day 1 · The Check-In
Personal, no agenda
A brief message — email or LinkedIn DM. References the previous conversation specifically. No pitch. Just reconnecting as a person. The goal is a reply, not a sale.
2
Day 5 · The Value Drop
Give before asking
Send something genuinely useful — a short insight on outsourcing ROI, a case study result, or a relevant article. No ask. This positions CELO as a thinking partner, not a vendor chasing a deal.
3
Day 10 · The Invitation
One specific, bounded ask
A direct invitation to a 30-minute "operations diagnostic" call. Not "let me know if you ever want to chat." Something specific: "I have two slots this week — 30 minutes, no obligation — to look at where outsourcing could free up your team." Easy to say yes to.
4
Day 16 · The Release
Close with dignity
"Totally understand if the timing isn't right. We'll be here when it is — and we've made some real improvements since we last spoke. Hope to connect when the time works." Closes the loop without burning the relationship. Often gets a reply.
Message Templates
For Past Proposals
"Hi [Name] — we spoke a while back about supporting your [team/function]. I wanted to check in — how are things going on that front? No agenda, just curious whether the challenge we discussed has evolved."
For Churned Clients
"Hi [Name] — I've been thinking about our time working together. I'd love to reconnect — partly to understand your experience, and partly because we've made some significant changes to how we work. Would you be open to a brief call? No pitch — genuinely just want to reconnect."
For Cold LinkedIn Connections
"Hi [Name] — we connected a while back and I don't think I ever followed up properly. I work with founder-led businesses in [US/UK/AU] that are scaling faster than their operations can keep up with. If that sounds familiar, I'd love to share what we do — 20 minutes, no obligation."
