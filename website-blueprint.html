Chapter 06 · Website Blueprint

From Careers Page
to Client Machine

The full content architecture for a website that works for CELO's revenue — not just its recruitment.

Current State vs. Required State

Current: celoph.com is a careers portal. The homepage leads with job listings, social media follows, and membership badges with no context. A potential client who lands on it has no idea what CELO does for businesses, what results they've delivered, or how to get started.

Required: A client-facing B2B website with a clear value proposition, service pages, social proof, a case study section, and a low-friction way to start a conversation. The careers section moves to a subdirectory (/careers) and stops leading the brand.

Homepage — Recommended Copy
Homepage Hero (Above the Fold)
Your business is ready to scale.
Your operations aren't. We fix that.
CELO Business Solutions builds dedicated remote teams for founder-led businesses in the US, UK, and Australia — handling customer support, back office, lead generation, and admin so your core team can focus on growth.
Book a Free 30-Min Diagnostic →

Below the hero: 3 trust badges (PHILCALL, Philippine Chamber of Commerce, PABC) with one line of context each. Then: "Trusted by businesses in [US / UK / AU]" with client logos if available, or industry icons if not.

Page Architecture

Six pages that convert strangers into clients

Page 1
Homepage
Hero → Trust signals → "Who we help" (ICP summary) → Services overview (3 cards) → One case study highlight → Testimonial → CTA to diagnostic call. Every section answers one question: "Why CELO over anyone else?"
Page 2
Services
Three service lanes, each with its own section:
  • Customer Support & CX — inbound/outbound, live chat, email, ticket management
  • Back Office & Admin — data entry, scheduling, document management, reporting
  • Sales Support & Lead Generation — prospecting, CRM management, follow-up sequences
Each section: what's included, who it's for, a result statement ("clients typically save X hours/week"). Ends with pricing context ("dedicated team from $X/month") and a CTA.
Page 3
Case Studies
The most important page CELO doesn't have yet. Each case study follows the same format: Industry → Challenge → What CELO did → Result (specific metric). Start with one — even an anonymised one. "A US-based e-commerce brand reduced their support ticket backlog by 73% in 60 days after building a dedicated CELO team." One real result is worth 10 capability claims.
Page 4
How We Work
The process page — answers "what happens after I say yes?" Four steps: Discovery call → Team matching → Onboarding (2 weeks) → Ongoing partnership. Removes fear and ambiguity. Include: SLA commitments, reporting cadence, account manager structure, what happens if a team member underperforms. Specificity = trust.
Page 5
About CELO
Not a founder bio — a mission statement about why CELO exists. "We believe that the best businesses in the world shouldn't be limited by what they can afford to hire locally." Membership bodies with proper context. Team photos if available. Philippine pride — not hidden, celebrated. The workforce IS the differentiator.
Page 6
Book a Diagnostic
A single-purpose landing page. Headline: "Tell us what's breaking in your operations. We'll tell you in 30 minutes if and how we can fix it." Calendly embed or simple contact form. No distractions, no navigation, no exit points. This page is the bottom of every funnel.
