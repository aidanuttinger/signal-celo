Primary ICP — The Scaling Founder
The Scaling Founder
Core client · Dedicated team retainer · Highest LTV · Easiest to retain
Company Profile
10–100 employees. $1M–$20M annual revenue. Growing faster than their team can keep up with.
Their Problem
Operations are breaking under growth. Founder is doing work that should be delegated. Hiring locally is too slow or too expensive.
Decision Maker
The founder or COO. Makes decisions quickly when they trust the solution. Has been burned by bad outsourcing before.
What They Need from CELO
A dedicated 2–5 person team embedded in their ops. Customer support, back office, admin, lead gen, or data management.

Buying Signals — They're Ready When They Say:

"We're spending too much time on tasks that aren't moving the needle"
"We tried a VA through Upwork but it was inconsistent"
"I need to hire 3 more people but I can't justify the cost in the US/UK"
"We need someone reliable — not a freelancer, a real team member"
Secondary ICP — The Operations Director
The COO / Ops Director
Mid-market · Larger team retainers · Higher deal value · Longer sales cycle
Company Profile
100–500 employees. $10M–$100M revenue. Already outsourcing some functions — evaluating consolidation or expansion.
Their Problem
Current BPO provider underperforming. Or scaling a successful pilot program. Needs a partner with documented processes and SLA accountability.
Decision Maker
COO or VP Operations. Has a formal evaluation process. Responds to case studies, references, and specific SLA commitments.
What They Need from CELO
Proven track record, documented QA processes, clear reporting, and a dedicated account manager. Proof that CELO has done this at their scale before.
Anti-Profile — Who to Pass On
Not CELO's Client

The price-only buyer — if the only question is "what's your hourly rate?", they'll leave the moment someone is $1/hour cheaper.  ·  Pre-revenue startups — no budget, no process, no ability to onboard and manage a team.  ·  Highly regulated industries without prior BPO experience (healthcare, legal, financial services at compliance-heavy scale) — these require specialist compliance infrastructure that needs to be built before accepting these clients.  ·  Clients who want a contractor, not a team — one-off projects with no intention of a retainer relationship. The economics don't work for either party.
