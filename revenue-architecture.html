Where the Money Is

A full audit of CELO's revenue model — and the path to predictable, scalable enterprise revenue.

The BPO Revenue Model

Three tiers. One missing.

Tier 1: Project-Based
$500–$5,000 per project
One-off tasks — data entry, virtual assistance for a campaign, short-term customer support coverage. Low commitment for the client, low value for CELO. High churn, high re-acquisition cost. Useful as a trial entry point, dangerous as a primary revenue model.
Tier 2: Dedicated Team Retainer
$3,000–$15,000/month
A committed team of 2–10 agents, fully embedded in a client's operations. Recurring, predictable, high-retention. This is the engine of BPO revenue. One client at $8K/month is better than 16 project clients at $500. CELO's growth lives in this tier.
Tier 3: Enterprise Partnership
$20,000–$100,000+/month
Large-scale outsourcing relationships with mid-market companies. Multi-department support, SLA-driven, often with custom tech integrations. Requires proven track record and case studies — the investment in Tier 2 client success is what unlocks Tier 3 relationships.
Opportunity Sizing

What the numbers look like

These aren't fantasy numbers. The Philippines BPO industry supports thousands of companies at this revenue level and beyond. The constraint isn't the market — it's the client acquisition system. Every deliverable in this session is designed to build that system.

Revenue Leaks

Where money escapes every month

No client-facing website means no inbound
Every month without a proper client website is a month of zero organic inbound leads. Prospects who find CELO through a referral or LinkedIn visit a careers page and leave. The cost of this is immeasurable and ongoing.
Referral-only acquisition has a ceiling
Word of mouth is unpredictable. It can't be forecast, managed, or scaled. Every business that relies on it eventually hits the ceiling of its founder's network — and stops growing there.
No packaged service tiers means every deal is bespoke
Custom proposals for every prospect create friction, delay, and inconsistency. Packaged tiers with clear scope and pricing let clients self-select, reduce sales cycle time, and signal professionalism.
No case studies means every sale starts from zero trust
In outsourcing, the biggest client objection is risk — "what if this doesn't work?" Case studies are the proof that eliminates this objection before it's raised. Without them, every sales conversation is an uphill trust-building exercise.
The Fix

The Revenue Priority Stack

Fix in this order: (1) Client-facing website with service pages and one case study. (2) Defined service tiers with pricing ranges. (3) Outbound system to LinkedIn decision-makers. (4) Reactivation campaign to dormant prospects. (5) Referral partner programme. Each step feeds the next. Start with the website — everything else points back to it.
